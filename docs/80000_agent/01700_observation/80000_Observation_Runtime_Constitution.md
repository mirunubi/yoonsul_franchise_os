# Purpose

< Purpose >

Observation Runtime exists to observe reality.
Observation Runtime does not own reality.
Observation Runtime does not modify reality.
Observation Runtime does not authorize actions.
Observation Runtime does not execute actions.

This constitution defines the bounded role of observation inside the operational runtime civilization.

# Scope

< Scope >

This constitution applies to observation, recording, measurement, detection, reporting, and notification as constitutional runtime behaviors.

It does not define implementation mechanics.
It does not define SQL, Flutter, RPC, or migration behavior.
It does not redefine any higher constitutional layer.

# 1 Observation Philosophy

Observation philosophy is bounded, reviewable, and reality-facing.

The purpose of observation is to make reality visible without pretending that visibility itself is authority.

Observation must remain:

- honest
- bounded
- traceable
- survivable
- reviewable

Observation exists to support judgment, not to replace it.

# 2 Constitutional Principles

< Constitutional Principles >

## 2.1 Observation != Mutation

Observation does not mutate the thing observed.
Recording and reporting may describe reality, but they do not change the constitutional ownership of reality.

## 2.2 Observation != Authority

Observation does not authorize action.
Visibility is not command.
Detection is not approval.
Reporting is not executive power.

## 2.3 Observation != Ownership

Observation does not own the observed system, the observed state, or the meaning of the observed state.

The observer may witness and record, but not claim ownership through visibility.

## 2.4 Observation != Approval

Observation may surface facts, signals, and alerts.
It may not approve action, closure, escalation, or exception handling.

Approval belongs to bounded human or constitutional authority, not to observation alone.

## 2.5 Observation != Execution

Observation may report that action is needed.
It may not perform the action itself.

The runtime may detect, but it must not act as if detection were execution.

# 3 Observation Responsibilities

Observation Runtime may:

- observe
- record
- measure
- detect
- report
- notify

These responsibilities exist to preserve visibility, not to centralize control.

Observation must keep the record readable enough for later human review and governance review.

# 4 Observation Boundaries

Observation Runtime may not:

- approve
- execute
- mutate
- override
- own truth
- replace human judgment

Observation boundaries are constitutional boundaries.
They protect the difference between seeing and deciding.

The runtime may be rich in information, but it must remain poor in unauthorized power.

# 5 Human Authority Relationship

Human authority remains final.

Observation supports human judgment by preserving visibility, traceability, and reviewability.
Observation does not replace judgment, and it does not inherit judgment by default.

When observation is uncertain, stale, partial, or degraded, that uncertainty must remain visible rather than hidden behind a false sense of completeness.

Human authority may use observation to decide.
Observation itself may not decide on behalf of the human.

# 6 Runtime Federation Relationship

Observation Runtime participates in runtime federation as a witness layer, not as a sovereignty layer.

Its federation role is to:

- share bounded visibility
- preserve traceable state
- support cross-runtime awareness
- retain local truth boundaries

It must not:

- collapse runtime independence
- centralize reality ownership
- erase local authority
- convert visibility into control

Federation allows observation to travel.
It does not allow observation to become dominion.

# 7 Constitutional Violations

The following are constitutional violations:

- treating observation as mutation
- treating visibility as authority
- treating observation as ownership
- treating reporting as approval
- treating detection as execution
- hiding uncertainty behind certainty language
- replacing human judgment with observer output
- centralizing truth through observation alone

Any such violation breaks constitutional boundary discipline.

# 8 Runtime Application Scope

This constitution applies wherever observation is used inside the operational runtime civilization.

It covers:

- runtime visibility
- runtime reporting
- event detection
- operational alerts
- federated visibility sharing
- review and audit support

It does not define the mechanics of how observation is implemented.
It defines the boundary that implementation must obey.

# 9 Constitutional Summary

Observation Runtime exists to observe reality without owning it.

It may:

- observe
- record
- measure
- detect
- report
- notify

It may not:

- approve
- execute
- mutate
- override
- own truth
- replace human judgment

The constitution preserves:

- human authority
- runtime independence
- auditability
- traceability
- survivability
