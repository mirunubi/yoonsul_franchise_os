<< 3rd cross-runtime interaction and boundary master constitution >>
<< federation-first cross-runtime governance worldview only >>
<< no implementation; no SQL; no Flutter; no RPC; no contracts >>

This document defines the global interaction doctrine, authority boundary rules, overlap governance, escalation propagation, survivability propagation, runtime coordination principles, and cross-runtime federation philosophy across the entire 3rd civilization runtime ecosystem.

AI and Physical AI are reserved future surfaces only; the current 3rd civilization is AI-compatible, not AI-dependent.

It is the master doctrine for how 3rd runtimes interact without silently stealing each other?䏭 ownership.

# 1 Runtime Federation Philosophy

< Federation Philosophy >

The 3rd civilization is a runtime federation, not a single fused runtime.

Runtimes may coordinate, observe, project, escalate, recover, and support each other, but they must not collapse into one another.

Principles:

- each runtime lane has a boundary.
- interaction does not imply ownership transfer.
- visibility can travel further than authority.
- recovery can travel further than execution.
- degraded coordination is still coordination.
- local survivability does not replace global sovereignty.

# 2 Interaction Does Not Mean Ownership Transfer

< Interaction Boundary >

Runtime interaction != ownership transfer.

Cross-runtime cooperation is normal. Silent authority migration is not.

Rules:

- a runtime may send context to another runtime.
- a runtime may receive visibility from another runtime.
- a runtime may escalate into another runtime?䏭 domain.
- none of these automatically transfer ownership.

If ownership changes, the change must be explicit, bounded, and constitutionally visible.

# 3 Propagation Doctrine

< Propagation >

The civilization must distinguish what propagates.

| propagation type | doctrine |
| ---------------- | -------- |
| visibility propagation != authority propagation | seeing something is not the same as governing it |
| escalation propagation != sovereignty replacement | escalation may seek help, not replace the owner |
| recommendation propagation != execution propagation | guidance is not action |
| projection propagation != operational truth mutation | projection should not rewrite truth |
| evidence propagation != approval propagation | evidence supports judgment but does not become it |

# 4 Cross-Runtime Interaction Principles

< Cross Runtime Relations >

## 4.1 Frontline Runtimes

Frontline runtimes include POS, Kiosk, KDS, DID, CMS, Waiting, Seating, Fulfillment, and Operational Agent behavior.

Principles:

- Frontline may project signals outward.
- Frontline may receive governance, supply, and recovery context inward.
- Frontline does not inherit HQ sovereignty by being visible.
- Frontline does not become supply truth, workforce truth, or HQ judgment by being operationally central.

## 4.2 Store Operations Runtimes

Store Operations runtimes include operational orchestration, workforce continuity, incident recovery, and local override handling.

Principles:

- Store Operations may coordinate across frontline and external lanes.
- Store Operations may interrupt and recover locally.
- Store Operations does not own payment settlement or HQ sovereignty.
- Store Operations is coordination authority, not universal authority.

## 4.3 HQ Runtime

HQ runtime is the governance, federation, visibility, and policy layer.

Principles:

- HQ may observe across stores.
- HQ may coordinate policy and escalation.
- HQ does not directly replace store-manager operational authority.
- HQ does not own local execution truth by observation alone.

## 4.4 Commerce Runtime

Commerce runtime is the customer relationship and commerce coordination lane.

Principles:

- Commerce may project to customer-facing surfaces.
- Commerce may coordinate membership and engagement.
- Commerce does not own POS transaction authority or operational recovery authority.
- Commerce is customer-facing federation, not customer truth sovereignty.

## 4.5 SCM Runtime

SCM runtime is the operational supply continuity and production coordination lane.

Principles:

- SCM may coordinate supply, ingredient, and manufacturing visibility.
- SCM does not own internal movement runtime.
- SCM does not own payment settlement, payroll, or HQ sovereignty.
- SCM is supply continuity, not the whole business.

## 4.6 WMS Runtime

WMS runtime is the physical movement and logistics continuity lane.

Principles:

- WMS may coordinate movement, staging, and transfer survivability.
- WMS does not own supply governance or supplier sovereignty.
- WMS does not own payment settlement or HQ sovereignty.
- WMS is logistics execution, not business judgment.

## 4.7 Supplier Runtime

Supplier runtime is the external supply relationship and reliability lane.

Principles:

- Supplier may coordinate external handoff, continuity, and risk.
- Supplier does not own inventory truth, payment settlement, or HQ sovereignty.
- Supplier is external relationship governance, not internal operational truth.

# 5 Authority-Boundary Doctrine

< Authority Boundaries >

The civilization must keep authority classes distinct.

| authority class | meaning |
| --------------- | ------- |
| transactional authority | owns order/payment transitions and transactional finality |
| operational authority | owns local human operational coordination and interruption handling |
| projection authority | owns what is shown, hinted, or staged to users |
| governance authority | owns policy, boundary, and federation rules |
| recovery authority | owns interruption, reconciliation, and survivability coordination |
| workforce authority | owns staffing continuity and human operational continuity |
| supplier authority | owns supplier boundary and external reliability coordination |
| logistics authority | owns movement, staging, transfer, and distribution continuity |

Rules:

- authorities may collaborate, but they do not auto-merge.
- one authority may inform another, but not replace it by default.
- any transfer across authority classes must be explicit and reviewable.

# 6 Overlap Governance Doctrine

< Overlap Governance >

Bounded overlap is allowed. Silent overlap is not.

Rules:

- bounded overlap means a runtime can touch another runtime?䏭 visibility without absorbing its ownership.
- no silent ownership mutation.
- no hidden authority escalation.
- no implicit runtime sovereignty transfer.

When overlap exists, the document trail must make the boundary legible to human readers and machine-assisted readers.

# 7 Survivability Propagation Doctrine

< Survivability Propagation >

Survivability must propagate through the federation.

Principles:

- degraded coordination continuity is preferable to silence.
- local fallback continuity is preferable to total interruption.
- manual-first survivability is preferable to brittle automation.
- text-first emergency continuity is preferable to uncertain rich state.

If one lane degrades, neighboring lanes should receive honest degradation signals rather than false certainty.

# 8 Emergency / Minimal Mode Doctrine

< Emergency Mode >

Emergency mode is a federation condition, not a shame condition.

Principles:

- degraded visibility handling must be explicit.
- stale-state visibility must be visible as stale.
- uncertainty visibility must be visible as uncertainty.
- operational continuity is preferred over silence.

Emergency mode must never present weak state as authoritative truth.

# 9 Human Final Authority Doctrine

< Human Authority >

The federation remains human-governed.

Principles:

- HQ governance authority remains explicit.
- store-manager operational authority remains explicit.
- bounded override is allowed where the lane constitution permits it.
- recovery reconciliation remains human-legible and human-supervised.

Human authority is not omnipotence.
It is bounded, visible, and lane-aware.

# 10 Future Federation Surfaces

< Future Surfaces >

Possible future federation surfaces include:

- distributed runtime federation.
- adaptive operational intelligence.
- cross-runtime anomaly propagation.
- operational civilization survivability.
- physical AI coordination readiness.

AI and Physical AI remain reserved future surfaces only; they are not current operational prerequisites.

These are future surface candidates, not implementation commitments.

# 11 Global Runtime Principles

< Global Principles >

The entire 3rd civilization must obey the following:

| principle | meaning |
| --------- | ------- |
| dismiss != resolved | a dismissal is not a fix |
| evidence != approval | evidence supports judgment but does not replace it |
| recommendation != execution | guidance is not action |
| visibility != authority | showing something is not the same as owning it |
| projection != operational truth | projection is a surface, not the source of truth |
| degraded operation > operational silence | weak continuity is better than none |
