This document defines runtime identity continuity, federation presence philosophy, bounded runtime/entity identity governance, and operational identity survivability for the operational runtime civilization.

It exists to prevent centralized identity domination, hidden impersonation, silent authority inheritance, and operational continuity corruption.

# 1 Runtime Identity Continuity Philosophy

< Identity Continuity >

Runtime identity must remain continuous enough to support federation, continuity, and judgment.

Principles:

- runtime identity continuity must be preserved.
- identity is bounded and observable.
- identity may support continuity, but it does not erase lane ownership.
- identity must survive degradation without pretending certainty.

# 2 Federation Presence Doctrine

< Presence >

Federation presence is the legible participation of a runtime in the wider operational civilization.

Principles:

- federation presence must remain bounded and observable.
- temporary presence is not permanent ownership.
- presence may support coordination, but it is not authority.
- uncertain presence must not appear authoritative.

# 3 Core Axioms

< Axioms >

The identity and presence doctrine obeys the following axioms:

| axiom | meaning |
| ----- | ------- |
| identity != authority | being identifiable does not grant control |
| visibility != authority | seeing a state does not own it |
| recommendation != execution | guidance is not automatic action |
| source of truth != master of all | a runtime may be locally authoritative without universal sovereignty |

# 4 Bounded Federation Identity Doctrine

< Federation Identity >

Federation identity must remain bounded so that the civilization does not collapse into a single center.

Principles:

- bounded federation identity must remain observable.
- runtime-local identity continuity must be preserved.
- identity coordination must not become identity domination.
- temporary override is not identity transfer.

# 5 Operational Identity Survivability Doctrine

< Survivability >

Identity must survive degraded and interrupted operation.

Principles:

- operational identity survivability must be preserved.
- emergency continuity identity doctrine must be explicit.
- degraded identity visibility must remain visible.
- stale presence visibility must remain observable.
- uncertain identity state must not appear authoritative.

# 6 Append-Only Identity Continuity

< Append Only >

Identity continuity must be preserved in a way that avoids silent rewriting.

Principles:

- hidden identity mutation is forbidden.
- silent authority inheritance is forbidden.
- append-only identity continuity must remain intact.
- append-only operational presence continuity must remain intact.

# 7 Cross-Runtime Identity Coordination

< Federation Coordination >

Identity may be coordinated across runtimes without being centralized.

Principles:

- cross-runtime identity coordination is permitted.
- coordination is not ownership.
- recovery identity continuity must remain observable.
- replay identity continuity must remain traceable.
- override identity continuity must remain bounded.

# 8 Lane-Specific Identity Survivability

< Lane Identity >

Different operational lanes may retain their own identity survivability needs.

Principles:

- fulfillment/operator identity continuity must remain preserved.
- workforce/runtime identity survivability must remain preserved.
- operational lanes may observe identity, but they must not silently inherit it.

# 9 Human Final Authority

< Human Authority >

Human judgment remains the final authority over identity-driven interpretation.

Principles:

- human final authority remains preserved.
- observability supports continuity but does not replace judgment.
- identity continuity may inform coordination, but it does not automatically execute action.
- authority commands downward; data flows upward.

# 10 Future Federation Surfaces

< Future >

The identity and presence layer may expand into broader federation surfaces.

Future surfaces:

- cross-store runtime identity
- distributed edge identity
- operational presence federation
- physical AI runtime identity
- civilization-scale runtime mesh

# 11 Governing Closing

< Closing >

The operational runtime civilization must remain itself while participating in the federation.

Principles:

- identity continuity is a support function, not a sovereignty function.
- presence is for continuity and coordination, not domination.
- hidden mutation and silent inheritance are forbidden.
- data flows upward, authority commands downward.
