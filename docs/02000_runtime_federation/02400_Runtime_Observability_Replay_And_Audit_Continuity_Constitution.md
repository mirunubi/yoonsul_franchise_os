This document defines runtime observability, operational replay, audit continuity, historical traceability, and federation-wide visibility preservation for the operational runtime civilization.

It exists to prevent surveillance authority, silent mutation, hidden replay rewriting, and audit ownership collapse.

# 1 Runtime Observability Philosophy

< Observability >

Observability is the ability to see runtime behavior clearly enough to support judgment.

Principles:

- observability is not authority.
- observability may support judgment, but it does not replace judgment.
- visibility must remain bounded and observable.
- degraded observability must remain visible rather than disguised as certainty.

# 2 Replay Continuity Doctrine

< Replay >

Replay is a bounded governance act for understanding operational history.

Principles:

- replay != mutation.
- replay must preserve historical truth.
- replay must not rewrite operational reality.
- replay interpretation may assist recovery, but it must not become hidden sovereignty.

# 3 Audit Continuity Doctrine

< Audit >

Audit is the continuity of evidence, chronology, and reviewability across runtime history.

Principles:

- audit != approval.
- evidence != approval.
- hidden audit deletion is forbidden.
- audit continuity must survive degradation, partition, and island-mode operation.

# 4 Core Axioms

< Axioms >

The observability and audit doctrine obeys the following axioms:

| axiom | meaning |
| ----- | ------- |
| visibility != authority | seeing a state does not own it |
| replay != mutation | replay does not rewrite the underlying runtime |
| audit != approval | review is not endorsement |
| evidence != approval | evidence supports judgment, not automatic acceptance |
| stale != authoritative | old visibility may exist, but it must not claim certainty |
| source of truth != master of all | a lane may be authoritative without universal control |

# 5 Operational Timeline Preservation Doctrine

< Timeline >

The operational timeline must remain legible across all federated runtimes.

Principles:

- event history must remain append-only.
- runtime event chronology must remain continuous.
- hidden replay rewriting is forbidden.
- runtime-local truth must remain preserved.
- uncertain replay interpretation must remain observable.

# 6 Cross-Runtime Observability Philosophy

< Federation Visibility >

Observability may travel across runtimes, but it must not erase their individuality.

Principles:

- cross-runtime observability may support coordination.
- cross-runtime observability does not transfer ownership.
- observability federation must remain bounded and reviewable.
- stale observability must not appear authoritative.

# 7 Replay Continuity Across Lanes

< Lane Replay >

Replay continuity must survive across the major operational lanes.

Principles:

- incident/recovery replay continuity must remain traceable.
- override replay continuity must remain append-only in spirit.
- fulfillment/recovery replay continuity must remain visible.
- inventory contamination replay continuity must remain historically traceable.
- workforce coverage replay continuity must remain observable.
- customer recovery replay continuity must remain auditable.

# 8 Operational Reality Preservation

< Reality >

Replay and observability exist to preserve operational reality, not to replace it.

Principles:

- replay must not rewrite operational reality.
- observability must not become surveillance authority.
- audit continuity must not become ownership collapse.
- runtime-local truth must remain bounded and preserved.

# 9 Degraded and Stale Visibility

< Degradation >

Observability may degrade, but it must not lie.

Principles:

- degraded observability must remain visible.
- stale observability must not appear authoritative.
- uncertain state must remain observable.
- uncertainty should be legible rather than hidden.

# 10 Human Final Authority

< Human Authority >

Human judgment remains the final authority over observability-driven interpretation.

Principles:

- human final authority remains preserved.
- observability supports judgment but does not replace judgment.
- replay findings may inform action, but they do not automatically execute action.
- authority commands downward; data flows upward.

# 11 Future Federation Surfaces

< Future >

The observability and audit layer may expand into broader federation surfaces.

Future surfaces:

- operational replay board
- cross-store timeline replay
- anomaly replay federation
- recovery simulation visibility
- physical AI replay interlock
- runtime civilization audit mesh

# 12 Governing Closing

< Closing >

The operational runtime civilization must preserve the legibility of its own history.

Principles:

- append-only history is safer than silent rewriting.
- observability is a support function, not a sovereignty function.
- replay is for understanding and recovery, not mutation.
- audit is for continuity and traceability, not ownership collapse.
- data flows upward, authority commands downward.
