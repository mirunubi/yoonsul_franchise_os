This document defines runtime boundary philosophy, operational containment doctrine, bounded propagation governance, and survivable operational isolation for the operational runtime civilization.

It exists to prevent authority bleed, silent cross-runtime contamination, hidden escalation propagation, and federation-wide operational collapse.

# 1 Boundary Philosophy

< Boundary >

Boundaries are legible separations for governance and survivability.

Principles:

- boundary != ownership.
- containment does not imply silence.
- isolation does not imply paralysis.
- boundaries must remain observable.

# 2 Core Axioms

< Axioms >

The boundary and containment doctrine obeys the following axioms:

| axiom | meaning |
| ----- | ------- |
| visibility != authority | seeing state does not own state |
| recommendation != execution | guidance is not automatic action |
| prediction != authority | forecasting does not grant control |
| source of truth != master of all | local truth may exist without universal domination |

# 3 Bounded Propagation Doctrine

< Propagation >

Propagation must remain bounded so that it does not turn into hidden sovereignty.

Principles:

- bounded propagation doctrine must remain explicit.
- runtime-local containment doctrine must remain explicit.
- propagation may support coordination, but it must not erase locality.
- hidden escalation spread is forbidden.

# 4 Operational Isolation Survivability

< Isolation >

Isolation is survivable when it is bounded, visible, and not mistaken for collapse.

Principles:

- operational isolation survivability philosophy must remain preserved.
- degraded containment survivability doctrine must remain preserved.
- false containment projection is forbidden.
- isolation is not parity with failure.

# 5 Containment of Cascades

< Cascades >

Certain failures can amplify across runtimes unless they are bounded.

Principles:

- replay contamination isolation doctrine must remain preserved.
- anomaly propagation containment doctrine must remain preserved.
- synchronization cascade containment doctrine must remain preserved.
- override propagation containment doctrine must remain preserved.

# 6 Containment of Leakage

< Leakage >

Leakage must be treated as a governance failure, not an acceptable convenience.

Principles:

- authority bleed prevention doctrine must remain preserved.
- memory leakage prevention doctrine must remain preserved.
- silent cross-runtime contamination is forbidden.
- federation-wide collapse amplification is forbidden.

# 7 Boundary Visibility and Uncertainty

< Visibility >

Boundaries may degrade in visibility, but they must not lie.

Principles:

- stale boundary visibility doctrine must remain observable.
- uncertain containment state must remain observable.
- hidden escalation propagation is forbidden.
- observability supports containment but does not replace judgment.

# 8 Append-Only Continuity

< Append Only >

Containment and isolation continuity must not be rewritten silently.

Principles:

- append-only containment continuity must remain intact.
- append-only isolation continuity must remain intact.
- hidden mutation of containment state is forbidden.
- silent propagation rewriting is forbidden.

# 9 Human Final Authority

< Human Authority >

Human judgment remains the final authority over containment-driven interpretation.

Principles:

- human final authority remains preserved.
- containment findings may inform coordination, but they do not automatically execute action.
- authority commands downward; data flows upward.

# 10 Future Federation Surfaces

< Future >

The boundary and containment layer may expand into broader federation surfaces.

Future surfaces:

- distributed runtime containment mesh
- anomaly quarantine federation
- physical AI operational isolation
- civilization-scale survivability zoning
- cross-runtime blast-radius governance

# 11 Governing Closing

< Closing >

The operational runtime civilization must keep failures from becoming contagious by default.

Principles:

- containment is a support function, not a sovereignty function.
- isolation is allowed if it remains visible and bounded.
- silent contamination and hidden escalation are forbidden.
- data flows upward, authority commands downward.
