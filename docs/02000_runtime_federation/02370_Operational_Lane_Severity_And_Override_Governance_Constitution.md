<< 3rd operational lane severity and override governance constitution >>
<< governance-first / operational-chaos aware worldview only >>
<< no implementation; no SQL; no Flutter; no RPC; no contracts >>

This document defines the governance philosophy for operational lanes, severity classification, bounded override authority, interrupt handling, escalation continuity, and cross-runtime operational governance.

It exists to prevent silent authority escalation, hidden override mutation, and centralized operational domination.

# 1 Operational Lane Philosophy

< Lane Philosophy >

Operational lanes are bounded governance surfaces, not ownership monocultures.

Principles:

- lanes coordinate operational reality.
- lanes may overlap, but they must remain legible.
- lanes are not the same thing as authority ownership.
- lane visibility does not create sovereignty.

# 2 Severity Governance Philosophy

< Severity >

Severity is a governance signal, not a blame assignment machine.

Principles:

- visibility != authority.
- recommendation != execution.
- evidence != approval.
- severity != blame assignment.
- anomaly != operational guilt.

Uncertain severity must not appear authoritative.
Stale severity visibility must remain observable.

# 3 Override Governance Philosophy

< Override >

Override is bounded governance action, not ownership transfer.

Principles:

- override != ownership.
- temporary override != permanent authority transfer.
- silent override mutation is forbidden.
- stale override visibility must remain observable.

Overrides may help the civilization survive, but they must remain visibly bounded.

# 4 Interrupt Handling Philosophy

< Interrupt >

Interrupts are governance signals that demand attention, not automatic sovereignty transfer.

Principles:

- interrupt handling must preserve continuity.
- interrupts may route attention, but they do not own the lane.
- emergency interrupt handling must remain bounded and reviewable.

# 5 Escalation Continuity Doctrine

< Escalation >

Escalation is continuity of concern, not forced mutation.

Principles:

- escalation != forced mutation.
- append-only escalation continuity is preferred.
- hidden escalation is forbidden.
- dismiss != resolved.

Escalation should preserve history while guiding humans toward the right authority.

# 6 Operational Lanes

< Lanes >

Operational lanes may include:

- NORMAL_OPERATION_LANE.
- RECOVERY_LANE.
- QC_LANE.
- FULFILLMENT_LANE.
- INTERRUPT_LANE.
- OVERRIDE_LANE.
- CUSTOMER_RECOVERY_LANE.
- INVENTORY_ANOMALY_LANE.

Rules:

- lane membership does not imply authority ownership.
- a lane can observe, route, or coordinate without owning the outcome.

# 7 Human Final Authority

< Human Authority >

Human final authority remains preserved.

Principles:

- humans decide.
- lanes recommend and surface severity.
- humans may accept, ignore, or override within bounded doctrine.
- the civilization remains human-governed.

# 8 Operational Conflict Visibility Doctrine

< Conflict >

Operational conflict must remain visible.

Principles:

- conflicting lanes should remain legible.
- cross-runtime interrupt survivability must be preserved.
- degraded governance continuity is preferable to silence.
- operational continuity > governance perfection.

The civilization should show conflict rather than conceal it.

# 9 Append-Only Override / Escalation Continuity

< Append Only >

Override and escalation continuity should remain append-only in spirit.

Principles:

- append-only override continuity is required.
- append-only escalation continuity is required.
- silent override mutation is forbidden.
- hidden escalation is forbidden.

The federation must remember how it intervened.

# 10 Runtime Governance Preservation

< Preservation >

The governance runtime must preserve:

- local authority isolation.
- bounded override doctrine.
- operational continuity.
- human final authority.
- cross-runtime survivability.

These are constitutional invariants, not optional features.

Possible future surfaces for this lane include:

These are future surface candidates, not implementation commitments.
