This document defines the federation-wide trust philosophy, bounded authority graph, runtime identity doctrine, authority precedence model, and operational trust survivability principles.

It exists to prevent centralized god-authority, silent authority escalation, hidden trust mutation, and operational ownership collapse.

# 1 Runtime Trust Philosophy

< Trust Philosophy >

Runtime trust is a bounded governance relationship, not a universal entitlement.

Principles:

- trust must be earned, bounded, and observable.
- trust may support coordination, but it does not erase lane ownership.
- trust is not the same thing as authority.
- trust should survive degradation without pretending certainty.

# 2 Bounded Authority Graph Doctrine

< Authority Graph >

Authority in the federation must be represented as a bounded graph, not a single omnipotent center.

Principles:

- authority must remain legible by lane and by class.
- authority boundaries must remain observable.
- authority graph edges may exist, but they must not become hidden sovereignty.
- no node may quietly become the master of all.

# 3 Federation Identity Doctrine

< Identity >

Federation identity is the ability of a runtime to remain itself while participating in the wider civilization.

Principles:

- runtime identity continuity must be preserved.
- federation identity must remain bounded and observable.
- local identity should not be erased by coordination.
- identity may participate in trust, but it must not become total ownership.

# 4 Core Axioms

< Axioms >

The federation trust doctrine obeys the following axioms:

| axiom | meaning |
| ----- | ------- |
| authority != ownership | authority in one context does not imply universal control |
| visibility != authority | seeing state is not the same as owning state |
| recommendation != execution | guidance is not automatic action |
| evidence != approval | evidence supports judgment but does not replace it |
| projection != truth | projected state is not operational reality |
| source of truth != master of all | a lane may be authoritative without total sovereignty |
| runtime-local truth doctrine | each lane may retain local truth within bounded scope |

# 5 Trust Survivability Doctrine

< Survivability >

Trust must survive weak links.

Principles:

- bounded trust survivability must be preserved.
- degraded trust federation is preferable to silence.
- operational survivability > centralized consistency.
- degraded operation > operational silence.

Trust should remain useful even when the federation is tired, stale, or partitioned.

# 6 Human Final Authority

< Human Authority >

Human final authority remains preserved.

Principles:

- humans remain the final authority.
- runtime trust cannot replace human judgment.
- human override remains bounded and visible.
- no trust relationship may erase human decision rights.

# 7 Authority Precedence Doctrine

< Precedence >

Authority precedence must be explicit.

Principles:

- precedence is a bounded ordering, not a universal hierarchy.
- precedence may guide conflict handling, not silently erase ownership.
- stale authority visibility must remain visible.
- uncertain authority must remain observable.

The federation should show who is leading the moment without pretending that leadership is permanent.

# 8 Override and Trust Mutation Doctrine

< Override >

Overrides may be necessary, but they must remain bounded.

Principles:

- hidden authority escalation is forbidden.
- silent trust mutation is forbidden.
- temporary override != permanent authority transfer.
- append-only override continuity is required.
- append-only authority continuity is required.

Overrides must remain visible enough for humans to reconstruct what happened later.

# 9 Cross-Runtime Trust Negotiation

< Negotiation >

Runtime trust is negotiated across lanes, not granted by a single hidden center.

Principles:

- cross-runtime trust negotiation must remain observable.
- operational truth conflicts must remain visible.
- authority conflicts must remain visible.
- trust negotiation must not collapse into hidden ownership transfer.

The federation should negotiate trust while preserving local ownership.

# 10 Emergency / Island-Mode Trust Continuity

< Emergency >

Trust continuity must survive federation damage.

Principles:

- emergency authority survivability must be preserved.
- island-mode authority continuity is allowed when the wider federation is impaired.
- stale authority visibility must remain visible.
- uncertain authority must not appear authoritative.

The federation may narrow under pressure, but it must not pretend that narrowing is wholeness.

# 11 Append-Only Continuity

< Append Only >

Authority and trust continuity should remain append-only in spirit.

Principles:

- append-only operational history is preferred.
- append-only authority continuity is required.
- append-only override continuity is required.
- silent mutation of trust history is forbidden.

The civilization must remember how trust and authority evolved.

# 12 Runtime Identity Continuity

< Identity Continuity >

Runtime identity must remain coherent over time.

Principles:

- runtime identity continuity is required.
- the federation should not dissolve local identity into a faceless center.
- runtime identity may be coordinated, but not erased.
