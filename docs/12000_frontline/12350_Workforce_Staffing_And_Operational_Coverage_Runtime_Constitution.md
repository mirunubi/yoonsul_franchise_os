<< 3rd workforce staffing and operational coverage runtime constitution >>
<< human-centered / survivability-first worldview only >>
<< no implementation; no SQL; no Flutter; no RPC; no contracts >>

This document defines Workforce, Staffing, and Operational Coverage Runtime as the bounded operational runtime responsible for staffing survivability, role continuity, emergency operational coverage, workforce visibility, and human-centered operational continuity.

It does so without turning workers into silent optimization resources or allowing automated operational authority reassignment.

# 1 Runtime Separation

< Separation >

Workforce Runtime is separate from:

- KDS Runtime.
- KDS Bridge Runtime.
- Agent Runtime.
- POS Runtime.
- Recovery Runtime.
- Fulfillment Runtime.

These runtimes may coordinate, but they must not collapse into one another.

# 2 Core Axioms

< Axioms >

The workforce doctrine obeys the following axioms:

| axiom | meaning |
| ----- | ------- |
| worker != operational resource abstraction | workers are human beings, not silent optimization units |
| staffing visibility != staffing authority | visibility does not confer assignment power |
| recommendation != reassignment | guidance is not automatic staffing mutation |
| visibility != authority | seeing state is not the same as owning state |
| evidence != approval | evidence supports judgment but does not replace it |
| operational shortage != worker fault | shortage is not automatic blame |
| anomaly != blame | anomaly is not proof of wrongdoing |
| overtime visibility != automatic approval | visibility is not approval |
| dismiss != resolved | dismissal is not resolution |

# 3 Workforce Runtime Responsibilities

< Responsibilities >

Workforce Runtime owns:

- staffing visibility.
- operational coverage visibility.
- role continuity visibility.
- emergency staffing coordination visibility.
- workforce survivability continuity.

It exists to keep human coverage visible, survivable, and dignity-preserving.

# 4 Workforce Runtime Boundaries

< Boundaries >

Workforce Runtime does not own:

- automatic workforce reassignment.
- silent role override.
- automatic punishment authority.
- payroll authority.
- final operational judgment.

Workforce may guide or coordinate, but it does not own these decisions.

# 5 Human-Centered Operational Philosophy

< Human Philosophy >

Workers are not silent optimization resources.

Principles:

- staffing must remain human-centered.
- human error does not equal moral failure.
- exhaustion and coverage pressure must remain visible.
- operational continuity must not come at the cost of hidden human overload.

This lane exists to keep people workable, not invisible.

# 6 Role Continuity and Emergency Coverage Doctrine

< Coverage >

Role continuity and emergency coverage must remain survivable.

Principles:

- temporary reassignment does not equal permanent authority transfer.
- emergency manager doctrine may be required.
- bounded remote-management doctrine may be required.
- role continuity survivability is part of the constitution.
- operational coverage survivability is part of the constitution.

Coverage is a way to keep the store alive, not a way to erase people?™s role boundaries.

# 7 Workforce Exhaustion and Degradation Doctrine

< Exhaustion >

Workforce exhaustion must remain visible.

Principles:

- workforce exhaustion visibility must remain explicit.
- staffing uncertainty must remain observable.
- stale staffing visibility must not appear authoritative.
- degraded staffing operation is preferable to silent overload.
- operational continuity > staffing perfection.

The lane should show strain honestly rather than pretending the team is fine when it is not.

# 8 Append-Only Workforce Continuity

< Append Only >

Workforce continuity should remain append-only in spirit.

Principles:

- staffing history must remain legible.
- role continuity history must remain traceable.
- silent override pressure must be resisted.
- hidden coverage mutation is anti-governance.

The lane must preserve how the team kept operating, not just the final surface.

# 9 Manual Intervention and Human Final Authority

< Human Authority >

Manual intervention remains preserved.

Principles:

- human final authority remains preserved.
- manager judgment may be required for emergency coverage.
- intervention must remain visible.
- evidence is not approval.
- recommendation is not execution.

The lane coordinates coverage, but it does not replace the human deciding who does what in the moment.

# 10 Runtime Coordination

< Coordination >

Workforce Runtime coordinates with:

- Agent Runtime.
- Store Operations Runtime.
- POS Runtime.
- KDS Runtime.
- HQ Runtime.
- Payroll Runtime.

This coordination helps preserve staffing survivability without transferring authority away from the relevant lane.

# 11 Future Federation Surfaces

< Future Surfaces >

Possible future surfaces for this lane include:

- staffing survivability federation.
- cross-store emergency coverage.
- operational fatigue visibility.
- workforce recovery coordination.
- human-error-aware staffing intelligence.

These are future surface candidates, not implementation commitments.

# 12 What Does Not Belong Here

< Out Of Scope >

Workforce Runtime does not own:

- automatic workforce reassignment.
- payroll authority.
- legal judgment authority.
- HQ sovereignty authority.
- customer identity authority.
- implementation details, SQL, Flutter, RPC, or contracts.

< Non-Goals >

This document does not define:

- tables, schemas, or storage models.

The job of this constitution is to define workforce governance, not the implementation.
